Commercial Loan

Traditional Purchase Financing

Build equity with every payment. At the end of your term, you own the truck outright with no balloon payments or buyout requirements.

  • Terms from 24 to 84 months
  • Fixed interest rates available
  • Build equity from day one
  • No mileage restrictions

  • Section 179 deduction eligible
Best For: Businesses planning long-term ownership, high-mileage operations, or those wanting to build fleet equity.

TRAC Lease

Terminal Rental Adjustment Clause

The commercial operator's preferred lease structure. Lower monthly payments than traditional financing with flexible end-of-term options and potential equity participation.

  • Lower monthly payments
  • Predictable residual value
  • Purchase option at lease end
  • Equity share if vehicle exceeds residual
  • Off-balance-sheet treatment possible
  • Ideal for commercial fleets
Best For: Commercial operators seeking lower payments, flexibility at term end, and potential upside from residual value.

Operating Lease

True Lease / Fair Market Value

Maximum flexibility with lowest monthly payments. Return the vehicle at lease end or purchase at fair market value. Ideal for businesses that refresh fleets regularly.

  • Lowest monthly payments
  • No residual risk
  • Return and upgrade easily
  • Off-balance-sheet financing
  • Maintenance packages available
  • Predictable total cost of operation
Best For: Companies prioritizing cash flow, wanting newest equipment, or preferring to refresh fleets every 3-5 years.

Fleet Line of Credit

Revolving Commercial Credit

Pre-approved purchasing power for growing fleets. Draw against your credit line as needed without repeated applications. Scale your fleet with your business.

  • Pre-approved credit limit
  • Draw as needed for purchases
  • Single application for multiple vehicles
  • Flexible repayment structures
  • Volume pricing benefits
  • Dedicated fleet account manager
Best For: Growing businesses adding multiple vehicles over time, companies wanting streamlined acquisition processes.

Section 179 Tax Advantages

The Section 179 deduction allows businesses to deduct the full purchase price of qualifying equipment—including commercial vehicles—in the year of purchase rather than depreciating over time. For Arizona businesses investing in commercial trucks, this can mean substantial first-year tax savings.

Key points for commercial truck buyers:

  • 2024 Limit: Up to $1,160,000 deduction on qualifying equipment purchases.
  • Vehicle Weight Matters: Trucks over 6,000 lbs GVWR (F-250 and above) qualify for higher deduction limits than lighter vehicles.
  • Business Use Required: Vehicle must be used more than 50% for business to qualify.
  • Same-Year Benefit: Take the deduction in the tax year the vehicle is placed in service.
  • Upfits Included: Service bodies, dump beds, and other upfits can be included in the deductible amount.

Consult your tax advisor for specific guidance on how Section 179 applies to your business situation. Peoria Ford's commercial team can provide vehicle specifications and documentation needed for tax purposes.
