Who Uses Ford Commercial Trucks in Colorado

Oil & Gas

Colorado's DJ Basin (Weld County) and Piceance Basin (Garfield County) are among the most active energy regions in the US. F-450 and F-550 service trucks — with Knapheide crane and service bodies — are the standard for oilfield service companies operating across the Front Range and Western Slope.

Construction

Colorado's population growth — especially in the Denver-Aurora metro, Colorado Springs, and Fort Collins — drives constant residential and commercial construction. Contractors need 4WD F-350 and F-450 trucks that handle mountain grade roads, unpaved access, and harsh winter conditions.

Agriculture & Ranching

Eastern Colorado's agriculture sector — wheat, corn, cattle — runs large fleets of F-350 and F-450 trucks. Western Slope orchards, livestock ranches, and irrigation operations all depend on heavy-duty 4WD pickup trucks for daily farm and ranch work.

Ski & Resort

Colorado's world-class ski resorts — Vail, Breckenridge, Aspen, Steamboat — run large maintenance and facilities fleets. F-250 and F-350 4x4 trucks with utility bodies handle lift maintenance, snowcat support, and resort operations in extreme mountain conditions.

Roofing

Colorado's frequent hailstorms — especially along the Front Range — make roofing one of the fastest-growing contractor categories. F-350 flatbed and dump trucks handle shingle delivery and tear-off removal for roofing crews working throughout the Denver metro and surrounding communities.

Government & Municipal

CDOT, Colorado counties, and mountain municipalities run significant Ford commercial fleets — especially F-350 and F-450 4x4 trucks with snowplow packages for road maintenance. Peoria Ford's BPN status provides government fleet pricing for qualifying Colorado entities.

4WD for Colorado
Why 4WD Matters for Colorado Commercial Trucks

Mountain Job Sites

Contractors working at elevation — mountain resort construction, highway maintenance, utility work — need 4WD capability for unpaved access roads, steep grades, and year-round job site conditions that would strand a 2WD truck.

Winter Operations

Colorado's Front Range receives significant snowfall from October through April. Commercial trucks without 4WD lose productive days sitting in lots or stuck on job sites. 4WD is effectively standard equipment for Colorado commercial operators.

Oilfield Access

Well pad roads in Weld County and Garfield County are often dirt, clay, or gravel — impassable for 2WD trucks in wet conditions. F-450 and F-550 4WD chassis cabs are the oilfield service standard across Colorado's energy regions.
