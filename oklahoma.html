Who Uses Ford Commercial Trucks in Oklahoma

Oil & Gas

The Anadarko Basin, SCOOP, and STACK plays make Oklahoma one of the nation's most active oilfield regions. F-450 and F-550 chassis cabs with IMT and Stellar crane/mechanics bodies are the standard service truck for Oklahoma oilfield operations — the 6.7L Power Stroke diesel is virtually universal.

Agriculture

Oklahoma's 34+ million acres of farmland support cattle, wheat, hogs, and poultry operations that run large F-350 and F-450 truck fleets. Farm and ranch trucks handle livestock trailers, equipment hauling, and daily field operations across Oklahoma's vast rural landscape.

Construction

Oklahoma City and Tulsa's continued growth drives residential and commercial construction demand. Contractors in both metros run F-350 and F-450 crews for framing, concrete, roofing, and specialty trade work throughout central and northeastern Oklahoma.

Pipeline & Midstream

Oklahoma's extensive pipeline infrastructure — gathering lines, transmission lines, and processing facilities — requires large service truck fleets for inspection, maintenance, and emergency response. F-450 and F-550 service trucks with specialized pipeline equipment are common throughout the state.

HVAC & Plumbing

Oklahoma's extreme temperature swings — hot summers and cold winters — create strong HVAC demand year-round. Service companies in OKC and Tulsa run F-250 and F-350 service body fleets for residential and commercial HVAC and plumbing service calls.

Government & Tribal

Oklahoma DOT, county governments, and Oklahoma's 39 federally recognized tribal nations run significant Ford commercial fleet accounts. Peoria Ford's BPN status provides government and tribal fleet pricing for qualifying entities.
