Build & Price Ford Work Trucks

Start with the chassis that matches your payload requirements. Configure cab style, engine (gas/diesel), drivetrain, and options. Key specs to consider:

  • GVWR: Total weight capacity (vehicle + cargo + passengers)
  • Payload capacity: Maximum cargo weight
  • Towing capacity: Maximum trailer weight
  • Cab-to-axle length: Critical for body installations

F-250: Class 2 (8,501–10,000 lbs GVWR). Best daily driver/work truck balance. Ideal for contractors who need capability without sacrificing ride comfort.

F-350: Class 3 (10,001–14,000 lbs). Available single rear wheel (SRW) or dual rear wheel (DRW). Heavy towing and hauling.

F-450: Class 4 (14,001–16,000 lbs). Chassis cab configuration for service bodies, flatbeds, and utility bodies.

F-550: Class 5 (16,001–19,500 lbs). Maximum Super Duty capability for the heaviest commercial applications.

Pro Tip: Spec one class higher than you think—payload disappears fast with upfits, tools, and materials.

7.3L Gas V8 Godzilla Engine:

  • Lower upfront cost ($8K–$12K less than diesel)
  • Simpler, less expensive maintenance
  • Best for under 20,000 miles/year
  • Excellent cold-weather reliability
  • 430 HP / 485 lb-ft torque

6.7L Power Stroke® Diesel:

  • Superior fuel economy (15–25% better)
  • Best for 25,000+ miles/year
  • Maximum towing capacity and torque
  • 200,000+ mile engine life expectancy
  • 475 HP / 1,200 lb-ft torque

Break-even point: 75,000–100,000 miles depending on fuel prices. If you tow heavy loads daily, diesel pays for itself faster.

Regular Cab: 2-door, 3-passenger. Maximum bed/body length. Best for solo operators who prioritize cargo space.

SuperCab (Extended Cab): 4-door, 6-passenger. Rear-hinged back doors. Good compromise between cab space and body length.

Crew Cab: 4-door, 5-6 passenger. Full-size rear doors. Best for crews who travel together. Most popular configuration.

GVWR (Gross Vehicle Weight Rating) is the maximum total weight your truck can safely carry, including:

  • Vehicle curb weight
  • Passengers
  • Cargo and materials
  • Upfit body weight
  • Fuel

Exceeding GVWR is illegal, voids warranties, and is extremely dangerous. It affects braking distance, tire wear, and suspension life.

CDL Requirement: Vehicles over 26,000 lbs GVWR require a Commercial Driver's License (CDL).

Upfitted Ford Trucks for Sale

An upfitted truck is a commercial vehicle modified with aftermarket bodies, equipment, or accessories to serve a specific trade or industry. Common upfits include:

  • Service bodies with lockable compartments
  • Van shelving and partitions
  • Dump bodies and hoists
  • Flatbeds and stake bodies
  • Cranes and lifting equipment
  • Refrigeration units

Upfitting transforms stock vehicles into purpose-built work machines ready to generate revenue on day one.

In-Stock Upfits: Pre-built units available for immediate delivery. Limited configurations but fastest to deploy.

Ship-Thru Program: Factory → Ford-approved upfitter → Dealer. Vehicle delivered complete with single warranty point. Most popular option for fleet buyers.

Dealer-Arranged: Buy the chassis, dealer coordinates with local upfitters. More customization options but longer lead times.

Customer-Arranged: Purchase chassis and manage upfit independently. Maximum flexibility but you coordinate everything.

  • Basic ($5,000–$10,000): Bed liner, toolbox, ladder rack, work lighting, backup camera
  • Trade-Specific ($10,000–$25,000): Full van shelving, partitions, ladder racks, power inverter, custom storage
  • Service Bodies ($25,000–$75,000): Full utility body, compartmentalized storage, integrated power, crane-ready
  • Specialty ($75,000–$250,000+): Bucket truck, crane truck, refrigerated body, mobile workshop

ROI Calculation: A properly upfitted truck can add 1–2 additional jobs per day = $50,000–$150,000+ annual revenue potential. Most upfits pay for themselves within 12–24 months.

  • Reading: Service bodies, enclosed bodies

Truck Body Types (Service, Flatbed, Dump)

A service body replaces the standard truck bed with an enclosed utility body featuring multiple lockable compartments on both sides and rear. Features include:

  • Steel or aluminum construction
  • Adjustable shelving systems
  • Fold-down work surfaces
  • Integrated LED lighting
  • Power inverter options
  • Crane/compressor mounting points

Best for: Plumbers, electricians, HVAC technicians, utility crews, mobile repair services, locksmiths.

F-350 with 9' dump body (2–3 cubic yards): Landscaping, residential projects, light hauling.

F-450 with 11' dump body (3–4 cubic yards): Commercial landscaping, light construction, municipal work.

F-550 with 12' dump body (4–5 cubic yards): Heavy commercial, roofing debris, larger municipal applications.

F-650/F-750 (5–16 cubic yards): Construction, excavation, heavy-duty hauling.

Important: Calculate by weight, not volume. Gravel weighs approximately 2,800 lbs per cubic yard. A truck rated for 4 yards of mulch may only handle 2 yards of gravel.

Flatbed: Open platform with no sides. Maximum versatility for oversized or irregularly shaped loads. Easy forklift access from any direction. Common for lumber, building materials, equipment transport.

Stake Body: Flatbed with removable vertical stakes/sides. Keeps loose materials contained. Stakes can be removed when not needed. Popular with landscapers, nurseries, and building supply delivery.

Stake with Liftgate: Adds hydraulic liftgate for heavy item delivery without dock access.

Service Body: Fully enclosed compartments on sides. Weather-protected storage. Higher security. Best for trades carrying expensive tools and parts.

Contractor Body: Open-top design with lower side compartments and open center cargo area. Easier to load large items. More versatile for mixed loads. Lower cost than full service body.

Many contractors start with a contractor body and upgrade to a service body as their tool inventory grows.

Ford Transit Commercial Vans

Low Roof (83.6" exterior height): Fits in standard parking garages and drive-thrus. Best for delivery, passenger transport, and applications where garage clearance matters.

Medium Roof (100.8" exterior height): Stand-up capability for most people. Good balance of interior space and exterior clearance.

High Roof (110.1" exterior height): Full stand-up height for 6'+ workers. Maximum interior space. Best for trade vans (HVAC, plumbing, electrical) where technicians spend significant time inside.

Most trade applications specify high roof for worker comfort and safety. Learn more about Transit configurations →

Short Wheelbase (130"): Best maneuverability. Urban delivery, mobile services.

Medium Wheelbase (148"): Popular all-around choice. Trade vans, contractor use.

Long Wheelbase (148" extended body): Maximum cargo length. Long materials, equipment hauling.

Extended Long Wheelbase (148" + extended body): Ultimate cargo capacity. Specialty conversions, mobile workshops.

Transit Cargo Van: Up to 4,650 lbs payload

Transit Chassis Cab: Up to 5,070 lbs payload

Transit Cutaway: Up to 5,210 lbs payload

Important: Subtract upfit weight from payload. A fully shelved and partitioned van loses 500–1,000 lbs of usable payload capacity. Always spec for actual loaded weight, not empty capacity. View full Transit specs →

Choose Transit if you:

  • Work primarily from the vehicle
  • Need weather-protected cargo
  • Carry smaller, organized inventory
  • Make multiple daily stops
  • Work in urban areas with parking constraints

Choose a work truck if you:

  • Haul heavy equipment or materials
  • Need towing capability
  • Require open-bed loading access
  • Transport large, bulky items
  • Work in off-road or rough terrain

Commercial Truck Financing & Leasing

Yes. Ford Pro FinSimple allows you to finance the complete vehicle—truck plus upfit—in a single loan. Benefits include:

  • Single monthly payment
  • Extended terms available (60–84 months)
  • Potential Section 179 tax deduction on complete vehicle
  • Preserves working capital
  • Simplified paperwork

This is the preferred method for most commercial buyers.

Commercial financing evaluates your complete business profile, not just personal credit score. Factors include:

  • Personal credit history
  • Business credit (if established)
  • Time in business
  • Monthly revenue and cash flow
  • Industry experience
  • Down payment amount

Strong cash flow can often qualify businesses even with imperfect credit. New businesses (under 2 years) typically need stronger personal credit or larger down payments.

Section 179 allows businesses to deduct the full purchase price of qualifying equipment in the year of purchase, rather than depreciating over multiple years.

  • 2024 Deduction Limit: $1,160,000
  • Qualifying Vehicles: Over 6,000 lbs GVWR (F-250 and larger)
  • Business Use Requirement: Must be 50%+ business use
  • Upfits Included: Body, equipment, and accessories qualify

Example: Purchase $85,000 upfitted F-450 in December, potentially deduct full amount from 2024 taxes.

Consult your tax advisor for specific guidance on your situation.

Buy (Finance) if you:

  • Plan to keep the vehicle 5+ years
  • Put high miles on vehicles
  • Want to build equity
  • Prefer ownership flexibility
  • Want to take Section 179 deduction

Lease if you:

  • Prefer lower monthly payments
  • Want newest equipment regularly
  • Have predictable, lower mileage
  • Value cash flow flexibility
  • Prefer off-balance-sheet financing

Term Length: 36–84 months (3–7 years)

Down Payment: 0–20% depending on credit profile

Interest Rates: Vary by creditworthiness, typically 5–12%

Mileage: No mileage restrictions on financed purchases

Ford Pro FinSimple often offers promotional rates and terms for qualified buyers. Fleet purchases (5+ vehicles) may qualify for additional incentives.

Upfitters, Warranties & Fleet Programs

QVM (Qualified Vehicle Modifier) is Ford's certification program for upfitters who meet strict quality and compliance standards:

  • Receive Ford-specific training
  • Follow official modification guidelines
  • Pass quality audits
  • Access Ford engineering support
  • Use approved components and methods

Using a QVM-certified upfitter ensures modifications are done correctly and won't void your factory warranty.

No—if done correctly. The factory warranty remains intact for properly installed modifications. Key points:

  • Use QVM-certified upfitters when possible
  • Keep all installation documentation
  • Upfit-related issues are the upfitter's responsibility
  • Ship-thru programs provide cleanest warranty protection

Ford cannot void your warranty simply because of an upfit—they must prove the modification caused the specific failure (Magnuson-Moss Warranty Act).

Base Warranty: 3 years / 36,000 miles bumper-to-bumper

Powertrain Warranty: 5 years / 60,000 miles

Diesel Engine: 5 years / 100,000 miles (Power Stroke®)

Corrosion: 5 years / unlimited miles (perforation)

Extended Service Plans: Available up to 8 years / 150,000 miles through Ford Protect.
