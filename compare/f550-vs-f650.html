Decision guide
F-550 or F-650 — Which Platform Is Right?

Choose the F-550 if…

  • ✓  Payload need is under 12,000 lbs
  • ✓  You need Super Duty serviceability and parts availability
  • ✓  Budget under $70,000 for the bare chassis
  • ✓  You want the 6.7L Power Stroke diesel reliability
  • ✓  Medium dump body (6–10 cubic yard) is the application
  • ✓  Contractor, landscaping, or utility company use

Choose the F-650 if…

  • ✓  Payload consistently needs to exceed 12,000 lbs
  • ✓  Large dump bodies (10–16 cubic yard) required
  • ✓  Heavy commercial hauling — gravel, fill dirt, asphalt
  • ✓  Refuse or recycling body applications
  • ✓  Dedicated commercial chassis durability required
  • ✓  Fleet operation with high annual mileage and heavy loads
Common applications
What Each Truck Is Built For

F-550: Heavy Contractor

The F-550 chassis cab is the standard platform for large Knapheide service bodies, crane trucks, and medium dump bodies for contractors, municipalities, and utility companies.

F-650: Commercial Hauling

The F-650 handles applications where the F-550 hits its limits — larger dump bodies, heavier payloads, and operations that need a true commercial-grade frame.

F-550: Mechanics Truck

The F-550 chassis is the standard platform for IMT and Stellar crane/mechanics truck bodies. The Power Stroke diesel provides reliable power for hydraulic crane systems.

Frequently asked
F-550 vs F-650 FAQ
Does the F-650 require a CDL?+
The F-650 at GVWR 25,999 lbs falls just under the 26,001 lb CDL threshold for Class 7 vehicles. Most F-650 configurations do not require a CDL for single-unit operation. However, when combined with a trailer (GCWR), a CDL may be required. Always verify requirements with your state DMV and DOT regulations.
What is the payload difference between F-550 and F-650?+
The F-550 chassis cab diesel is rated for up to 12,750 lbs of payload. The F-650 significantly exceeds this — capable of 18,000+ lbs depending on configuration and spec. For most contractors, the F-550 payload covers their needs. The F-650 becomes necessary for heavy construction hauling, large dump bodies, or refuse applications.
Which is easier to service and maintain — F-550 or F-650?+
The F-550 benefits from the vast Super Duty parts and service network — every Ford dealer services it, and parts are widely available. The F-650 uses a different commercial chassis platform and Cummins diesel, which requires dealers with medium-duty truck service capability. For fleets with their own maintenance, both are manageable. For general business owners, F-550 serviceability is a meaningful advantage.
