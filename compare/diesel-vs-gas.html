Which Engine Is Right for Your Business?

Choose Diesel (6.7L) if…

  • ✓ You tow over 15,000 lbs regularly
  • ✓ You drive 25,000+ miles per year
  • ✓ You need maximum payload capacity
  • ✓ You plan to keep the truck 5–10 years
  • ✓ Long-term total cost of ownership matters

Choose Gas (7.3L) if…

  • ✓ Towing stays under 15,000 lbs most days
  • ✓ Annual mileage is under 20,000 miles
  • ✓ Lower upfront cost is a priority
  • ✓ Simpler maintenance and no DEF system
  • ✓ You prefer gasoline infrastructure
Common questions
Diesel vs Gas FAQ
Does diesel pay back its premium over gas?+
At 25,000+ annual commercial miles, diesel typically pays back its $10,000–$12,000 premium within 3–4 years through fuel savings, then delivers lower cost-per-mile through the life of the truck. At lower mileage (under 15,000/year), the payback period extends beyond typical ownership cycles and gas becomes the more economical choice. Diesel also commands $5,000–$10,000 higher resale value, which factors into the real-world cost analysis.
Is DEF (diesel exhaust fluid) a hassle?+
DEF adds a running cost of approximately $0.02–0.04 per mile and requires occasional refilling (every 3,000–5,000 miles in most commercial applications). Most commercial operators fill DEF at the same intervals as fuel top-offs, and most gas stations carry it. For fleets, DEF bulk delivery is available. It's not a significant operational burden for most commercial buyers, but it's a real cost to factor into your operating budget.
Is the 7.3L gas engine reliable for commercial use?+
Yes. Ford's 7.3L V8 is a purpose-built commercial engine — not adapted from a consumer platform. It's known for reliability, simpler maintenance, and strong performance for light-to-medium commercial work. For businesses not regularly pushing maximum tow or payload ratings, the 7.3L is an excellent commercial engine that will provide years of dependable service at lower upfront and maintenance cost than diesel.
