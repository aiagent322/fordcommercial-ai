Common questions
F-350 vs F-450 FAQ
What is the difference between the F-350 and F-450?+
The F-450 has a higher GVWR (16,500 vs 14,000 lbs), more payload capacity, and higher max gooseneck towing at 40,000 lbs vs 35,750 lbs. The F-450 also offers the High Output 6.7L Power Stroke diesel (500hp/1,200 lb-ft) not available on F-350. The F-450 is exclusively Crew Cab and DRW — no lighter cab or SRW options.
Is the F-450 worth the upgrade from F-350?+
For heavy commercial use — large dump bodies, crane trucks, regular towing near 35,000 lbs — yes. The F-450 chassis is purpose-built for demanding upfits. For moderate commercial use, the F-350 DRW chassis cab covers most needs at lower cost.
Which is better for a dump truck — F-350 or F-450?+
Light-to-medium dump bodies (4–6 cubic yard) work on both F-350 and F-450 chassis cabs. For larger commercial dump bodies (7–10 cubic yard) or heavy-duty hydraulic systems, the F-450 or F-550 chassis is the professional standard due to higher GVWR and chassis strength.
