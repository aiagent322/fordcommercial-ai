Which Van Wins for Your Business?

Plumbing & HVAC

Transit wins. Larger US upfit ecosystem for Adrian Steel and Ranger Design shelving. Better dealer access for service in the field. Lower total cost.

Electrical Contractors

Transit wins. Multiple roof heights, excellent interior organization options, and nationwide dealer support make Transit the standard for electricians.

Long-Haul Delivery

Sprinter edge. The 170" wheelbase Sprinter's 530 cu ft cargo volume is best-in-class for maximum cargo van delivery — especially for taller, bulkier loads.

Last-Mile Logistics

Transit wins. Lower cost, easier to source, and the largest US commercial van service network. Amazon, FedEx, and UPS all use Transit extensively.

Mobile Workshops

Transit wins. Adrian Steel, Ranger Design, and Sortimo shelving systems for Transit are more widely available and better supported in the US market.

Specialty / Luxury

Sprinter edge. Medical, luxury shuttle, and specialty conversion market has a stronger Sprinter ecosystem — more conversion shops and premium options.

Frequently asked
Transit vs Sprinter FAQ
Is the Ford Transit better than the Mercedes Sprinter?+
For most US commercial buyers, the Transit offers lower total cost of ownership, a dramatically larger dealer and service network, and an equally strong upfit ecosystem. The Sprinter has advantages in maximum cargo volume and European market refinement. Transit has been America's best-selling commercial van for 47 consecutive years for good reason — it's the practical, cost-effective choice for the majority of commercial operations.
How does Transit compare to Sprinter on cargo volume?+
The Transit High Roof 148" wheelbase delivers 487.3 cubic feet of cargo space. The Sprinter High Roof 170" wheelbase delivers approximately 530 cubic feet — about 9% more volume. If maximum cargo space is your primary requirement and the Sprinter's higher cost and service network limitations are acceptable, the Sprinter's long wheelbase wins on raw volume. For most commercial applications, the Transit's 487 cubic feet is more than sufficient.
