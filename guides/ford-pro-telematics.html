Key features
How Fleet Managers Use Ford Pro Intelligence

GPS & Location Tracking

See every vehicle's real-time location on a single map. Review trip history, identify inefficient routing, and verify that drivers are staying within assigned territories. Geofencing sends alerts when vehicles enter or leave defined areas after hours.

Driver Behavior Scoring

Each driver receives a safety score based on hard braking, rapid acceleration, speeding, and sharp cornering. Fleet managers use scores to coach drivers, reduce accident risk, and lower insurance premiums. Studies show behavior monitoring alone reduces fuel costs 5–10%.

Fuel Consumption Analytics

Track fuel usage per vehicle, per driver, and per route. Identify trucks with declining fuel economy — often the first sign of maintenance issues. Spot idling patterns that waste fuel without productive miles. Most fleets recover the subscription cost in fuel savings alone.

Predictive Maintenance

Ford Pro Intelligence monitors engine data in real time and alerts fleet managers to developing issues before they become breakdowns. DTC (Diagnostic Trouble Code) notifications arrive before a check engine light appears — giving you time to schedule service on your terms, not the road's.

Maintenance Scheduling

Oil change intervals, tire rotations, brake inspections, and filter replacements are tracked per vehicle based on actual mileage and engine conditions — not just calendar dates. Fleet managers see a dashboard of upcoming maintenance across the entire fleet.

Third-Party Integration

Ford Pro Intelligence integrates with fleet management platforms including Fleetio, ARI, Wheels, and Donlen. API access allows custom integrations with dispatch software, payroll systems, and job costing tools used by contractors and service companies.

Frequently asked
Ford Pro Intelligence FAQ
What vehicles are compatible with Ford Pro Intelligence?+
Ford Pro Intelligence is built into 2022 and newer Ford commercial vehicles equipped with SYNC 4 — including the F-150, F-250, F-350, F-450, F-550, and Transit. Older vehicles can participate in the Ford Pro ecosystem using compatible aftermarket telematics hardware. Ask Peoria Ford which aftermarket solutions integrate with the Ford Pro dashboard for mixed-age fleets.
Can I try Ford Pro Intelligence before committing?+
Yes. Ford offers trial periods for Pro Intelligence subscriptions on qualifying new vehicle purchases. New Ford commercial vehicles from Peoria Ford typically include a trial period of the Enhanced or Pro tier — giving fleet managers hands-on experience before committing to a subscription. Ask Peoria Ford's commercial team about current trial offers when ordering your truck.
How does Ford Pro Intelligence compare to Samsara or Geotab?+
Ford Pro Intelligence has a key advantage over third-party telematics: it integrates directly with the vehicle's native systems rather than relying on an OBD plug-in. This means deeper diagnostic access, more accurate fuel data, and no hardware to install or maintain. Samsara and Geotab offer broader multi-brand fleet support and more mature reporting platforms — making them better suited for fleets mixing Ford with other brands. For all-Ford fleets, Pro Intelligence is the most seamless choice.
