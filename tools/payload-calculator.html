Common mistakes
What Buyers Get Wrong on Payload

Forgetting body weight

A full-size steel service body weighs 1,400–1,800 lbs before a single tool goes in. Subtract body weight first, then calculate usable cargo capacity.

Confusing GVWR with payload

GVWR is the total vehicle weight limit. Payload is GVWR minus curb weight — much smaller. A 14,000 lb GVWR F-350 weighs ~6,500 lbs empty, leaving only ~7,500 lbs of actual payload.

No safety margin

Running at 100% rated payload daily accelerates wear on brakes, suspension, and frame. Build in 15–20% — or step up to the next model if you regularly approach the limit.

Frequently asked
Payload FAQ
How do I calculate my payload requirement?+
Add every pound the truck will carry: cargo + passengers (200 lbs each) + tools + body/upfit weight. Multiply by 1.15 for a 15% safety margin. The result is your minimum payload requirement — choose a truck whose rated payload exceeds this number. The calculator above does this automatically.
Is the window sticker payload the same as the door jamb sticker?+
No. The window sticker shows maximum payload for the highest-payload configuration of that model. Your door jamb sticker shows the actual rated payload for your specific truck as built — accounting for cab, options, and installed equipment. It may be lower than the advertised maximum. Always use the door jamb number for compliance purposes.
