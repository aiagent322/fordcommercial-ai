Who Uses Ford Dump Trucks

Landscaping

Mulch, topsoil, gravel, and stone delivery. F-350 DRW with 2–3 cubic yard dump is the landscaper's workhorse — the right capacity for residential and commercial jobs without oversizing.

Construction

Dirt, debris, and spoil removal from job sites. F-450 or F-550 handles heavier construction loads. Double as a materials delivery truck on the way in.

Asphalt & Paving

Hot mix asphalt delivery and pothole patching requires a dump truck that can keep materials warm in transit. Insulated beds available on F-450 and F-550 platforms.

Demolition

Debris and demolition waste removal. F-550 with reinforced dump body handles the weight and abrasion of concrete, masonry, and metal scrap.

Agriculture

Grain, feed, and farm waste hauling. F-350 or F-450 dump trucks handle most farm operations while remaining maneuverable on rural property.

Snow Removal

Dump beds double as salt spreader platforms. F-350 and F-450 dump trucks are popular in northern markets for winter road operations.

Common questions
Ford Dump Truck FAQ
What Ford truck is best for a dump body?+
The F-350 DRW chassis cab is the most popular light-duty dump platform for landscaping and light construction — compact enough to navigate job sites, capable enough for daily 2–3 cubic yard loads. For heavier construction, asphalt, or demolition work, the F-450 or F-550 provides more payload and larger body capacity. Both are available through Peoria Ford with upfitter installation included.
What is the payload capacity of a Ford dump truck?+
Payload depends on the chassis and body weight. F-350 chassis cab: up to 7,850 lbs payload minus the dump body weight (approximately 1,200–1,800 lbs) = net payload of ~5,500–6,500 lbs. F-450: up to 8,000 lbs gross payload. F-550: up to 12,750 lbs — the most capable in the Super Duty lineup for dump applications.
Do Ford dump trucks require a CDL?+
F-350, F-450, and F-550 dump trucks fall under Class 3–5 (GVWR 14,000–19,500 lbs) and do not require a CDL in most states. CDL requirements begin at Class A (26,001+ lbs combined) or Class B (26,001+ lbs single vehicle). Always check your state's specific requirements, especially if towing additional trailers.
