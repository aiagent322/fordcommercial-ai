The KUV — America's most popular service body
Knapheide KUV Service Body

The Knapheide Utility Vehicle (KUV) body is the most specified commercial truck body in the United States. Built for professional tradespeople who need organized, secure, weatherproof storage for tools and equipment — the KUV is the de facto standard for plumbers, electricians, HVAC technicians, and contractors.

Available in multiple lengths (8 ft, 9 ft, 10 ft, 11 ft), compartment configurations, and steel or aluminum construction. The KUV fits Ford F-350 and F-450 chassis cabs as the standard platform.

Knapheide has manufacturing plants across the US, meaning parts and service are available nationally — an important consideration for fleets operating across multiple regions.

Trades That Run Knapheide Bodies

Plumbing

The F-350 chassis cab with Knapheide KUV steel service body is the industry standard for plumbing service fleets. Organized compartments for pipe, fittings, valves, and tools keep plumbers productive all day.

HVAC

HVAC contractors run Knapheide KUV bodies on F-250 and F-350 chassis. The compartment system accommodates refrigerant recovery equipment, gauges, copper line, and replacement parts for commercial service accounts.

Electrical

Electrical contractors use Knapheide bodies for wire reels, conduit, test equipment, and hand tools. The KUV aluminum body is popular with electricians who need to maximize payload while keeping the truck organized.

General Contracting

The Knapheide KPTE contractor body — taller sides, more top storage — is built for general contractors who carry power tools, lumber, and mixed materials on commercial job sites.

Municipalities

City and county public works departments specify Knapheide aluminum utility bodies on F-450 and F-550 chassis for parks, roads, and facilities maintenance — durable, corrosion-resistant, and US-made.

Utilities

Electric, gas, and telecom utilities run Knapheide aluminum utility bodies for their field service crews. The aluminum construction withstands the rigors of daily outdoor use and provides long service life for fleet operators.

Is Knapheide better than Reading or other service body brands?+
Knapheide and Reading are both excellent US-made commercial body manufacturers. Knapheide has the largest market share and most extensive dealer/service network nationwide. Reading Truck Body is strong in the Northeast and mid-Atlantic regions. For most buyers, the choice often comes down to local upfitter relationships and specific body configurations available. Peoria Ford works with both manufacturers to source the right body for your application.
Should I get steel or aluminum Knapheide body?+
Steel KUV bodies cost roughly $5,000–$8,000 less and are better for heavy contractor abuse where dents and dings are inevitable. Aluminum KUV bodies are 500–700 lbs lighter (important for payload), corrosion-resistant for wet or coastal climates, and preferred by utilities and municipalities who keep trucks for 10+ years. For most tradespeople, steel is the practical choice. For fleet operators tracking payload and longevity, aluminum pays back the premium.
