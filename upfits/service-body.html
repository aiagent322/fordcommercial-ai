Common questions
Service Body Truck FAQ
What Ford truck is best for a service body?+
The F-350 chassis cab (SRW or DRW) is the industry standard for commercial service bodies. It provides a dedicated commercial frame, up to 7,850 lbs payload, and wheelbase options to accommodate 8–11 ft bodies. For heavier applications or longer bodies, the F-450 chassis cab adds payload and frame capacity. The F-250 can accept a light service body but is not available as a chassis cab.
How much does a service body upfit cost?+
Service body upfits add $8,000–$18,000 to the chassis cost, depending on size, material (steel vs aluminum), and compartment configuration. A Knapheide 9-foot steel body on an F-350 chassis runs approximately $10,000–$14,000 installed. Full-spec builds with crane, liftgate, or custom compartments can reach $25,000+. Peoria Ford handles chassis + body as a single purchase.
Steel vs aluminum service body — which should I choose?+
Steel is more durable and lower cost upfront — the choice for heavy-duty contractors. Aluminum is lighter (saving 400–800 lbs of payload for cargo), rust-resistant, and preferred in coastal states or for HVAC and plumbing trades where payload efficiency matters. Most Knapheide and Reading bodies are available in both materials.
