Enclosed Cargo Van FAQ
Ford Transit vs a pickup truck — which is better for a tradesperson?+
It depends on your work. If you carry tools and parts but rarely tow and work in urban areas, the Transit's enclosed cargo space, better fuel economy, and easier parking often make it the more practical choice. If you tow trailers, haul heavy materials, or work on rural job sites, a pickup truck with service body is more versatile. Many tradespeople run one of each — a Transit for tech service calls and an F-350 for heavy hauling days.
Does the Ford Transit require a CDL?+
No. The Ford Transit's GVWR ranges from 8,550 to 10,360 lbs — well under the 26,001 lb CDL threshold. Any licensed driver can operate a Transit cargo van without a commercial driver's license, which is a major advantage for businesses hiring general drivers for delivery routes.
