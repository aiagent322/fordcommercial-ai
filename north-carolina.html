Top industries in North Carolina
Who Uses Ford Commercial Trucks in North Carolina

Construction

Charlotte's ongoing commercial and residential construction boom — plus growth in the Triangle, Triad, and Wilmington — keeps NC contractors among the busiest in the Southeast. F-350 and F-450 crew cabs and chassis cabs are standard across general contracting, framing, and specialty trade crews.

HVAC & Plumbing

NC's hot, humid summers and mild winters create year-round HVAC demand. Service companies across Charlotte and the Triangle run F-250 and F-350 service body fleets for new construction and residential service calls statewide.

Landscaping

North Carolina's four-season climate and rapid suburban growth drive strong landscaping demand. F-250 and F-350 trucks with trailers serve residential and commercial landscaping companies from the mountains to the coast.

Agriculture

Eastern NC's agricultural belt runs significant F-350 and F-450 fleets for hog, poultry, and tobacco operations. Western NC's apple orchards, Christmas tree farms, and livestock ranches depend on 4WD commercial trucks for daily operations.

Logistics & Delivery

Charlotte's airport cargo hub and the Triangle's e-commerce distribution growth drive strong Transit cargo van demand. Last-mile delivery companies and B2B distributors run Ford Transit fleets throughout NC's metro areas.

Roofing

NC's hurricane exposure and ice storm activity create consistent roofing contractor demand. F-350 flatbed and dump trucks are standard in roofing fleets operating across the coastal plain and Piedmont.
