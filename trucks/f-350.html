Single Rear Wheel vs. Dual Rear Wheel

One of the most important decisions when specifying an F-350 involves choosing between single rear wheel (SRW) and dual rear wheel (DRW) configurations. Each serves distinct commercial purposes, and Peoria Ford's commercial specialists help Arizona businesses select the optimal configuration for their specific operations.

The single rear wheel F-350 provides exceptional payload and towing capability while maintaining the narrower profile that simplifies parking and maneuvering in urban construction sites across the Phoenix metro area. For contractors who need to access tight residential work zones or navigate crowded commercial parking areas, the SRW configuration delivers F-350 capability in a more manageable package.

The dual rear wheel configuration—commonly called the "dually"—maximizes stability under heavy loads and provides the ultimate platform for fifth-wheel and gooseneck towing. Arizona horse haulers, RV towers, and heavy equipment transporters gravitate toward the DRW F-350 for its planted, confident feel when pulling substantial trailers across desert highways and mountain passes. The wider rear track distributes weight more effectively, reducing sway and improving braking performance under load.

Power Stroke Diesel Performance

While the 7.3-liter gas V8 serves admirably in lighter-duty F-350 applications, the 6.7-liter Power Stroke turbodiesel transforms this truck into an unstoppable commercial force. The diesel's 1,050 lb-ft of torque—available at just 1,800 RPM—provides effortless acceleration even when climbing Arizona's steepest grades with maximum loads. The engine's exhaust brake reduces wear on the service brakes during descents, extending brake life and reducing maintenance costs for fleets operating in mountainous terrain.

Ford's decade-plus refinement of the current Power Stroke architecture has produced exceptional reliability records. Arizona commercial operators report six-figure mileage without major engine work when following Ford's recommended maintenance schedules. The diesel's superior fuel economy under load often results in fuel savings that offset the higher purchase price within 18-24 months of typical commercial operation.
