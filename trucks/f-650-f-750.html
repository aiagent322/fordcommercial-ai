F-650 vs. F-750: Choosing Your Platform

The F-650 operates as a Class 6 commercial vehicle with GVWR ratings up to 26,000 pounds. This classification positions it perfectly for regional delivery operations, municipal applications, and commercial vocations where gross weight requirements fall below Class 7 thresholds. Many Arizona businesses select the F-650 specifically because its Class 6 rating allows operation without CDL requirements in certain configurations, simplifying driver hiring and compliance.

The F-750 steps up to Class 7 territory with GVWR ratings reaching 37,000 pounds. This additional capacity serves heavy-haul applications, larger box truck configurations, and demanding vocational work where payload requirements exceed F-650 specifications. Towing companies favor the F-750 for its ability to recover and transport larger vehicles, while construction firms appreciate its capacity for carrying heavy equipment and materials.

Powertrain Options for Every Application

Unlike lighter Ford trucks that limit engine choices, the F-650 and F-750 offer multiple powertrain configurations to match specific operational requirements. The 6.7-liter Power Stroke turbodiesel provides proven reliability and exceptional low-end torque for applications requiring maximum pulling power. The 7.3-liter gasoline V8 serves operations where fuel infrastructure favors gasoline or where lower initial cost takes priority.

Ford also offers the F-650 and F-750 with the 7.3-liter V8 configured for propane autogas conversion, providing Arizona businesses with alternative fuel options that can significantly reduce operating costs. Municipal fleets and environmentally-conscious operators increasingly specify propane-capable trucks to meet sustainability goals while maintaining commercial capability.

Medium Duty Cab Configurations

Ford engineers the F-650 and F-750 with cab configurations tailored to commercial requirements. The standard Regular Cab positions the driver close to the front axle for improved maneuverability in tight quarters—essential for urban delivery operations navigating Phoenix streets and loading docks. The SuperCab adds rear-hinged doors and additional interior space for crew transport or secure storage of valuable equipment and documents.

The Crew Cab configuration provides full rear doors and seating for additional crew members, serving construction companies and utility contractors who transport complete work crews to job sites. All cab configurations share the same commercial-grade interior appointments, including vinyl flooring that withstands years of work boot traffic and controls positioned for drivers wearing work gloves.

Commercial Chassis Engineering

The F-650 and F-750 chassis utilize fully-boxed ladder frame construction rated for commercial duty cycles. Unlike pickup-based platforms adapted for heavy use, these medium duty frames are engineered from the ground up for continuous commercial operation. Frame rail options accommodate various body mounting requirements, while multiple wheelbase configurations ensure proper weight distribution for different body lengths.

The front axle specifications support the heavier gross weights medium duty applications demand, with appropriate spring and brake packages included from the factory. Rear axle options include multiple ratio choices and differential configurations to match operational requirements, from high-speed highway delivery to slow-speed vocational work requiring maximum pulling power.
