The Foundation of Commercial Capability

The Ford F-250 Super Duty represents the entry point into Ford's legendary Super Duty lineup, delivering the perfect balance of capability, maneuverability, and value for commercial operators across Arizona and the Southwest. Whether you're running a construction crew in Phoenix, managing a landscaping fleet in Scottsdale, or hauling equipment across the desert to remote job sites, the F-250 provides the muscle and reliability your business demands.

At Peoria Ford, we've seen thousands of Arizona businesses build their operations around the F-250. From HVAC contractors who need to tow heavy equipment trailers to plumbers requiring service body upfits, the F-250's versatility makes it the most popular commercial truck in our inventory. The truck's 22,000-pound maximum towing capacity handles everything from gooseneck trailers loaded with machinery to fifth-wheel campers for those weekend escapes after a hard week of work.

Powertrain Options Built for Work

Ford offers two powerhouse engine options in the F-250, each engineered for specific commercial applications. The standard 7.3-liter V8 gas engine delivers 430 horsepower and 485 lb-ft of torque through a proven architecture that Arizona mechanics know inside and out. This engine excels for businesses that prioritize lower upfront costs, easier maintenance, and operations that don't require the ultimate in torque.

For operators who demand maximum pulling power and fuel efficiency under load, the legendary 6.7-liter Power Stroke turbodiesel remains the commercial standard. With 475 horsepower and a staggering 1,050 lb-ft of torque, the Power Stroke transforms the F-250 into a true commercial workhorse capable of hauling heavy loads across Arizona's mountain passes without breaking a sweat. The diesel's superior fuel economy under load often justifies its higher initial cost within the first year of commercial operation.
