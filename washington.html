Who Uses Ford Commercial Trucks in Washington State

Technology & Logistics

Amazon, Microsoft, Boeing, and tech-adjacent companies in the Seattle metro run significant Ford Transit fleets for facilities maintenance, shuttle operations, and last-mile delivery. The Puget Sound's dense urban geography makes Transit the practical choice for many operations.

Agriculture

Eastern Washington's Columbia Basin — apples, wheat, potatoes, hops, and wine grapes — is one of the most productive agricultural regions in the US. F-350 and F-450 4WD trucks handle large-scale irrigation farming, orchard operations, and farm equipment support.

Forestry & Timber

Washington's timber industry — Olympic Peninsula, Cascades, and eastern forests — relies on F-350 and F-450 trucks for harvest support, road maintenance, and forestry operations. 4WD is essential for logging road access in mountainous terrain.

Construction

Western Washington's ongoing residential and commercial construction boom — driven by tech-sector population growth — keeps contractors busy throughout the Puget Sound. F-350 and F-450 crew cabs and service body trucks are standard on Seattle-area job sites.

Utilities & Telecom

Puget Sound Energy, Pacific Power, and Washington's rural electric cooperatives run large Ford commercial fleets. Telecom companies serving western Washington's dense metro market also run significant Transit and F-350 service fleets.

Government & Municipal

Washington DOT, King County, and major municipalities run significant Ford fleet accounts. Port of Seattle and Port of Tacoma also operate commercial vehicle fleets. Peoria Ford's BPN status provides government fleet pricing for qualifying WA entities.
